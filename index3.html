VACCINES:

Vaccines for Dogs

    Core Vaccines (Recommended for all dogs):

  • Rabies: Protects against rabies, a fatal disease that can be transmitted to humans. Rabies vaccination is required by law in many areas.
  • Distemper: A serious, often fatal viral disease affecting the respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems.
  • Parvovirus (CPV): A highly contagious virus that causes gastrointestinal illness in puppies and unvaccinated dogs.
  • Canine Parainfluenza: Often combined with other vaccines, it protects against respiratory infections.

    Non-Core Vaccines (Recommended based on risk factors):

  • Bordetella bronchiseptica (Kennel Cough): Commonly given to dogs that spend time in kennels, dog parks, or daycare.
  • Leptospirosis: A bacterial infection that can affect the liver and kidneys; it's more common in areas with standing water or wildlife.
  • Lyme Disease: A vaccine against the bacteria transmitted by ticks, recommended for dogs in high-risk areas.
  • Canine Influenza (CIV): Protects against a contagious respiratory disease, particularly important in areas where outbreaks are common.

Vaccines for Cats:

    Core Vaccines:

  • Rabies: Essential for cats, especially those that go outdoors, as they can transmit rabies to humans and other animals.
  • Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis (FVR): Part of the FVRCP vaccine, protects against herpesvirus, which causes respiratory illness.
  • Calicivirus (FCV): Another component of FVRCP, calicivirus causes respiratory infections and oral diseases.
  • Panleukopenia (FPV): A severe and often fatal viral infection similar to parvovirus in dogs, included in the FVRCP combination.

    Non-Core Vaccines:

  • Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV): Recommended for outdoor cats or cats exposed to infected cats, as it suppresses the immune system and can lead to other health problems.
  • Feline Immunodeficiency Virus (FIV): Optional and typically administered based on risk factors such as outdoor exposure.
  • Chlamydia felis: Protects against respiratory infections, often given in multi-cat households or shelters.

Vaccines for Other Pets:

    Rabbits:

  • Myxomatosis: A virus spread by fleas and mosquitoes, common in outdoor rabbits.
  • Rabbit Hemorrhagic Disease (RHDV): A deadly disease affecting the liver and other organs

    Ferrets:

  • Distemper: Canine distemper can be fatal for ferrets, so vaccination is important.
  • Rabies: Especially important for ferrets that interact with other animals or go outdoors.
